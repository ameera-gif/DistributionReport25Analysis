1What Happened

  • Malaysia and Palestine spent 92.9% of the year's budget. The other 5 branches shared 7%.
  • One program, Qurban Care for Ummah, was 41% of the entire year's spending, in just 3 months.
  • Palestine reached the most people (~138,000), but has the weakest paperwork.
  • USA, Australia, Turkiye stayed tiny, under 0.3% combined. Unclear if that's the plan or just underfunding.

3What We Don't Actually Know Yet

  • Whether the ~236,000 figure is real unique people, or the same people counted more than once.
  • Whether Malaysia/Palestine's spend split was a choice, or just where donations happened to be earmarked.
  • Our overhead cost ratio, cash reserves, and revenue by source, none of this exists yet.
  • Why some costs (like several Palestine and MEA items) are the exact same amount every month regardless of scale.
Bottom line: real work happened in 2025, but we can't yet prove it was efficient, sustainable, or measured accurately.

4By Department

MarketingGiving is as seasonal as spending. Build off-season campaigns, and set separate success metrics for USA/Australia/Turkiye instead of judging them like relief branches.
FinanceExplain the repeated flat costs in Palestine and MEA. Then produce revenue, overhead ratio, and cash reserve figures, the most overdue numbers in this report.
OperationsSet one real method for avoiding double-counted beneficiaries. Stop comparing branches to each other; compare each program to its own target instead.
